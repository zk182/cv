CV Checklist
⛔️ The following steps apply once you have completed the LinkedIn checklist for content and text.

🧩 Format

-Use a template.
-Companies in the USA prefer LaTeX-style CVs.
-Creative designs submitted in Word lower the quality of your CV and can even lead to rejection.
-It must be one page long.

📄 Main Content

-Your CV should be consistent with your LinkedIn profile, but not identical.
-Any discrepancies will create doubts, and doubts tend to lead to rejection.
-Edit your CV according to the company you are applying to:
-Look at LinkedIn profiles of people who work at the company and copy them; these are the "winners."
-Change job titles, content, messages, and skills to better match what the company is looking for. You want to tell a story that highlights the main strengths of your profile.

[Recommended] Add an introduction or "About Us" section tailored to each company.

This introduction should explicitly or implicitly answer the question, "Why should company XXX hire me?"

Every time you edit the content, run it through Grammarly; typos in your CV are unacceptable.

❌ What you shouldn't do:

-Create your own templates or use outdated tools like Word.
-Avoid "spray and pray" strategies (using the same generic CV for all your applications).
-Adding images and photos.
-Having more than one page.
-Using an email address like @hotmail or @outlook. It's preferable to create a Gmail account and set up a redirect if you use another email client. Do not use stars, bars, scores, or -descriptors to express your skill level or experience.
-Include a link to a GitHub profile where you have no projects, significant contributions, or activity.
